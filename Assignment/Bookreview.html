Book Review

The Great Gatsby

  • character-8/10
  • story-9/10
  • overall-8.5/10

F. Scott Fitzgerald’s The Great Gatsby is a timeless classic that explores themes of decadence, idealism, and excess in the Jazz Age. The novel tells the story of Jay Gatsby's unrequited love for Daisy Buchanan, set against the backdrop of wealth and extravagance on Long Island. The intricate character development and the critique of the American Dream make it a must-read.

To Kill a Mockingbird

  • character-10/10
  • story-10/10
  • overall-10/10

Harper Lee’s To Kill a Mockingbird is a profound and moving novel that addresses serious issues such as racial injustice and moral growth. Through the eyes of young Scout Finch, readers experience the prejudices and moral complexities of the American South in the 1930s. The characters, especially Atticus Finch, are deeply memorable and inspiring.

1984

  • character-9/10
  • story-9.5/10
  • overall-9.25/10

George Orwell’s 1984 is a dystopian novel that explores the dangers of totalitarianism and extreme political ideology. The story follows Winston Smith, a man who lives in a society controlled by the Party and its leader, Big Brother. Orwell’s depiction of surveillance, propaganda, and individualism makes 1984 a chilling and thought-provoking read.
